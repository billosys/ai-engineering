Concourse Index

Here at *Practical Typography* you may have noticed the snazzy numbered list <span class="no-hyphens">bullets:</span>

1.  Like

2.  These

3.  Here

<div id="yes-i-did" class="subhead">

[Yes I did](#yes-i-did)

</div>

These are provided by a special font called Concourse Index that I derived from my family. With OpenType <a href="ligatures.html" class="xref">ligatures</a> activated, you get double <span class="no-hyphens">digits:</span>

<div class="ten">

1.  From <span class="no-hyphens">eleven</span>

</div>

<div class="fortyone" style="font-feature-settings: 'ss01'">

1.  To <span class="no-hyphens">forty-two</span>

</div>

<div class="fiftyfive" style="font-feature-settings: 'ss02'">

1.  And <span class="no-hyphens">fifty-six</span>

</div>

<div class="ninetyeight" style="font-feature-settings: 'ss03'">

1.  Through <span class="no-hyphens">ninety-nine</span>

</div>

<div id="amazing--tell-me-more" class="subhead">

[Amazing—Tell me more](#amazing--tell-me-more)

</div>

The default figures are inside white circles. Other styles are included as <a href="opentype-features.html" class="xref">OpenType features</a>:

<div class="no-reset" style="font-feature-settings: 'ss01'">

1.  With stylistic set 1, you get white <span class="no-hyphens">squares.</span>

</div>

<div class="no-reset" style="font-feature-settings: 'ss02'">

1.  With stylistic set 2, black <span class="no-hyphens">circles.</span>

</div>

<div class="no-reset" style="font-feature-settings: 'ss03'">

1.  And with stylistic set 3, black <span class="no-hyphens">squares.</span>

</div>

<div id="fantastic--how-much" class="subhead">

[Fantastic—How much?](#fantastic--how-much)

</div>
