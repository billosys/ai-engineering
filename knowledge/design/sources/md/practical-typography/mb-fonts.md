I appreciate the small payments for *Practical Typography*. But readers who buy fonts are the ones who really keep this book alive—and by extension, let me keep doing this as my job. If you’ve already done so—thank <span class="no-hyphens">you!</span>

If you haven’t—consider it! As I explain in <a href="font-recommendations.html" class="xref">font recommendations</a>, changing out your fonts is a fast and effective way to upgrade your typographic standards. Especially if you’ve been on a lifelong diet of <a href="system-fonts.html" class="xref">system fonts</a>. Live a <span class="no-hyphens">little.</span>

For text & display, I offer three serif families (, , and ), two sans serif families ( and <a href="hermes-maia.html" class="xref">Hermes Maia</a>), one semi-serif family (), and , a monospaced family. You can try them any time by using the font picker at the bottom of the page. I also offer <a href="advocate.html" class="xref">Advocate</a>, an all-caps font for headlines, titles, and <span class="no-hyphens">logos.</span>

For prices and buying links, click any font name <span class="no-hyphens">above.</span>
