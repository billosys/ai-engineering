One of the most popular foundations for recent text families has been the historical model known as *Scotch*, from the mid-19th century. One example is Georgia, which was designed primarily to work well on computer screens of the 1990s. But 20 years on, its design compromises are becoming obsolete. <a href="fontrec/miller.html" class="xref">Miller</a>, by contrast, shares Georgia’s Scotch influence but with more subtlety and detail. (See <a href="system-fonts.html" class="xref">system fonts</a> for a closer comparison.) <a href="fontrec/harriet.html" class="xref">Harriet</a>, <a href="fontrec/chronicle.html" class="xref">Chronicle</a>, and —the last is my design—are also based on the Scotch model but add contemporary details and <span class="no-hyphens">flavor.</span>
