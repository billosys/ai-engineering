Typographic humor

Professional typographers, like copy editors and grammarians, sometimes have a reputation for being humorless nitpickers. Certainly, the nature of the job requires some nits to be <span class="no-hyphens">picked.</span>

But humorless? On the contrary—the more you’ve trained your eye for typography, the more acutely you detect the continual butchery of our written language. For typographers, to look away would amount to dereliction of duty. But to constantly take umbrage would put us into a state of moral paralysis. Therefore, humor is necessary for <span class="no-hyphens">survival.</span>

1.  [**The LTypI photo collection**](http://www.flickr.com/groups/ltypi/)  
    LTypI stands for “Lack of Typographic Imagination”. This site collects instances where designers have chosen fonts that match the text in a perhaps too obvious way. [I’m a contributor,](https://www.flickr.com/groups/ltypi/pool/53061055@N05) of <span class="no-hyphens">course.</span>

    Seen in Hollywood, California. The font used for BoHo is a 1910 design called [Hobo.](https://fontsinuse.com/typefaces/4015/hobo)

2.  [**2,002 Honest Fonts**](https://www.somethingawful.com/fakesa/fontsite/)  
    I return to this site every year or so. Without fail, I literally cry from laughing so hard. How is this possible? I can only conclude that, like [“Flash Gordon”](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MS4_Z84-rRE) by Queen, this is a perfect work of <span class="no-hyphens">art.</span>

3.  [**Fuck Yeah Typeface Design**](https://fuckyeahtypefacedesign.tumblr.com/)  
    I honestly have no idea who runs this. But it punctures typographic pretentions with glee—and, as the name signals, some vulgarity. [My favorite,](https://fuckyeahtypefacedesign.tumblr.com/post/97682581639) however, is suitable for all ages. Beware: some *very* inside-baseball humor here. Further beware: if you get all these jokes, you have reached peak typography <span class="no-hyphens">nerd.</span>

4.  [**Typography 2020: a special listicle for America**](typography-2020.md) and [**Typography 2024: for America! for America’s best**](typography-2024.md)  
    My own contributions to this burgeoning <span class="no-hyphens">genre.</span>
