The Baskerville <a href="system-fonts.html" class="xref">system font</a> is mediocre: brittle and excessively quaint. It’s a poor representation of the work of John Baskerville, an 18th-century English printer who improved typefounding with his innovations in paper, ink, and printing. The best recreation of Baskerville’s own fonts is <a href="fontrec/baskerville-10.html" class="xref">Baskerville 10</a>. Though <a href="fontrec/ingeborg.html" class="xref">Ingeborg</a> and <a href="fontrec/kingfisher.html" class="xref">Kingfisher</a> don’t directly resemble Baskerville’s type designs, they do share the crisp and bright look that Baskerville helped <span class="no-hyphens">popularize.</span>
