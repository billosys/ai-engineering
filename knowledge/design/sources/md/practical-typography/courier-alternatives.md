I’m in an awkward position. As your typography advisor, I’ve counseled you not to use <a href="monospaced-fonts.html" class="xref">monospaced fonts</a>.

But the truth is—I really like them. The golden age of monospaced fonts was probably the 1950s, when IBM led the typewriter industry and released a series of great monospaced designs. One of these was Courier. But the <a href="system-fonts.html" class="xref">system font</a> Courier New is a beastly imitation of the original: spindly, lumpy, and just plain <span class="no-hyphens">ugly.</span>

As an antidote, I’ve created , a monospaced font family influenced by several typewriter fonts of the ’50s, and optimized for <a href="body-text.html" class="xref">body text</a>. Triplicate has genuine italic styles (instead of a sloped roman like Courier) and small caps <span class="no-hyphens">too.</span>

<a href="fontrec/pitch.html" class="xref">Pitch</a> and <a href="fontrec/input.html" class="xref">Input</a> show there’s still room for design exploration in monospaced fonts. <a href="fontrec/fira-mono.html" class="xref">Fira Mono</a> is also very nice, and one of the few <a href="free-fonts.html" class="xref">free fonts</a> I recommend without <span class="no-hyphens">reservation.</span>
