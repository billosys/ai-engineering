Like Georgia, Verdana was designed for the computer screens of the 1990s, so it’s great for the websites of the 1990s. For everything else, it’s not so great. <a href="fontrec/alright-sans.html" class="xref">Alright Sans</a> is known as a *humanist* sans because it relies on the proportions of traditional serif fonts. Sweet Sans, on the other hand, shares Verdana’s pleasing width, and would be a good choice for <a href="business-cards.html" class="xref">business cards</a> or <a href="letterhead.html" class="xref">letterhead</a>. Likewise <a href="fontrec/breuer.html" class="xref">Breuer</a>, which is slightly condensed, and has more of a geometric <span class="no-hyphens">feel.</span>

I designed and <a href="hermes-maia.html" class="xref">Hermes Maia</a> based on letterpress faces of the early 20th <span class="no-hyphens">century.</span>
