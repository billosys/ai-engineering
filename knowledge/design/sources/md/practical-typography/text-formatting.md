Text formatting

Specimen of fonts offered by William Caslon, <span class="no-hyphens">1728.</span>

When I tell people I write about typography, they often say, “Oh, you mean <span class="no-hyphens">fonts?”</span>

Yes and no. Sure, fonts are an important part of typography. But there’s much more to typography than <span class="no-hyphens">fonts.</span>

Similarly, there’s more to text formatting than picking a font. Formatting includes everything that affects the appearance of the words on the page—not just fonts, but also point size, bold and italic styling, and <span class="no-hyphens">letterspacing.</span>

In this chapter, there aren’t as many bright lines between correct and incorrect habits as in the last chapter. You won’t be able to get by on rote application of rules. You’ll need to start making typographic judgments of your <span class="no-hyphens">own.</span>

(And if you’re looking for the good fonts, they’re in the next chapter—<a href="font-recommendations.html" class="xref">font recommendations</a>.)
