Typography 2024: for America! for America’s best

This year’s slate of 11 Republican candidates for president seems like an amuse-bouche compared to 2020, when there were [27 Democratic <span class="no-hyphens">candidates.</span>](typography-2020.md)

I relied on the NYT to tell me [who’s running for <span class="no-hyphens">real.</span>](//nytimes.com/interactive/2023/us/politics/presidential-candidates-2024.html)

To reveal one of my most contrarian opinions: I’ve never understood the recurring critique of American presidential politics that we have *too many* candidates. (Sample alarmist think-piece headline: [“Too Much Democracy Is Bad For Democracy”.](https://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/2019/12/too-much-democracy-is-bad-for-democracy/600766/)) What’s the ish? That’s why it’s called an election. Besides, if you want to carp about the unintended consequences of too much choice, why not point your laser beams at the [17th amendment](https://www.senate.gov/about/origins-foundations/senate-and-constitution/seventeenth-amendment.htm) to the US Constitution? A lot more could be said about *that*, I assure <span class="no-hyphens">you.</span>

Even more confusing is the quadrennial [hand-wringing](https://nymag.com/intelligencer/2023/06/cornel-west-no-labels-joe-manchin-third-party-biden-spoiler-candidate-2024-election.html) about so-called “spoiler candidates”, a pejoration that keeps slouching toward normalized use, sort of like “frivolous lawsuit”. Let’s keep the blame for “spoiling” any election where it belongs—with the people who <span class="no-hyphens">voted.</span>

Largely, however, I think these theories are promoted by political journalists as a means of protecting their own hoary narratives of presidential politics. Here’s mine: It’s chaotic. It’s weird. Nobody knows anything. Thus, evaluating the candidates through the design & typography of their campaign websites is as valid a method as any. If you think otherwise, you’re a typographic <span class="no-hyphens">spoiler.</span>

<div id="overall-impressions" class="subhead">

[Overall impressions](#overall-impressions)

</div>

The websites of the [2020 candidates](typography-2020.md) were not miracles of originality. Though even compared to that middling standard, the 2024 candidates have converged even more forcefully around a small set of typography, color, and navigation <span class="no-hyphens">choices.</span>

The red–blue contrast problem: this wordmark looks like BID <span class="no-hyphens">N</span>

As for color—though the designers of the US flag realized that red and blue have poor contrast and thus shouldn’t overlap each other, presidential candidates continue to struggle with this unavoidable optical <span class="no-hyphens">reality.</span>

I recognize that the point of these websites is 1) to raise money and 2) … huh? what? Still, I’m mystified by the complete lack of interest in distilling the candidate’s pitch to any kind of concrete argument. In an election, isn’t the major question “Why you and not the other one?” Why can no one answer this? The average Doritos advertisement makes a better <span class="no-hyphens">case.</span>

“Well, different voters have different preferences.” Nah, I don’t believe it. I absolutely could win in 2028. My slogan: “More money for everyone. And nacho cheesier flavor <span class="no-hyphens">too.”</span>

Until then, however, these are your <span class="no-hyphens">choices.</span>

<div id="democratic-candidates" class="subhead">

[Democratic candidates](#democratic-candidates)

</div>

<div id="joe-biden" class="subhead">

[Joe Biden](#joe-biden)

</div>

[](//joebiden.com)

Biden’s reelection site is largely a step down from [his very good 2020 effort.](typography-2020.html#joe-biden) The fonts have been upgraded to the excellent [Decimal](https://www.typography.com/blog/introducing-decimal) and the Caslon-inspired [Frame.](https://commercialtype.com/catalog/frame) Otherwise, however, it’s just weird: the cream-colored background, the Mortal Kombat-inspired tagline *finish the job*, the scribbles on the page purporting to be Joe’s handwriting, and new wavy stripes in the Biden wordmark reminiscent of Andrew Yang’s [baconesque 2020 logo.](typography-2020.html#andrew-yang) A quintessential example of what happens in design projects when a large budget is handed over to a large committee: by trying to be everything to everyone, it avoids being anything to <span class="no-hyphens">anyone.</span>

<div id="marianne-williamson" class="subhead">

[Marianne Williamson](#marianne-williamson)

</div>

[](//marianne2024.com)

A perplexing entry for those hoping that Williamson’s return to presidential politics would be even wilder [than 2020’s.](typography-2020.html#marianne-williamson) Williamson’s website is devoid of visual interest, even compared to other 2024 candidates—and a major step down from the [arty self-portrait](typography-2020.html#marianne-williamson) she relied on last time. If you’re Marianne Williamson, why aren’t you swinging for the fences? Acting presidential was never going to be your lane. The fonts are [Poppins](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Poppins) (which she used in 2020 too) and [Faustina.](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Faustina)

<div id="robert-kennedy-jr" class="subhead">

[Robert Kennedy Jr.](#robert-kennedy-jr)

</div>

[](http://kennedy24.com)

The only good thing about Robert Kennedy’s [campaign](https://apnews.com/article/robert-kennedy-jr-presidential-campaign-9fb5ed5c8e1fd31d2a4458d44b086593) is the lacerating *Curb Your Enthusiasm* episode that it will eventually inspire. The fonts are nice—[Freight Sans](https://fonts.adobe.com/fonts/freight-sans) and [Source Serif](https://fonts.adobe.com/fonts/source-serif) (which I [have recommended](free-fonts.md))—and so far haven’t killed <span class="no-hyphens">anyone.</span>

<div id="republican-candidates" class="subhead">

[Republican candidates](#republican-candidates)

</div>

<div id="donald-trump" class="subhead">

[Donald Trump](#donald-trump)

</div>

[](//donaldjtrump.com)

I doubt that any future biography of Donald Trump will note that his 2020 campaign manager, [Brad Parscale,](https://www.forbes.com/sites/denizcam/2017/12/06/from-web-designer-to-trumps-digital-confidant-brad-parscale/?sh=a2c8b316e9e4) started out as his 2016 web designer. Sad! No idea who’s pulling the strings in 2024. Given Trump’s preoccoupation with at-least-basic-cable production values, the shoddy photo editing and other signs of corner-cutting are a surprise. (Given Trump’s [legendary vendor-stiffing,](https://www.salon.com/2022/08/26/trump-never-pays-his-bills-truth-social-reportedly-stiffs-contractor-amid-financial-disarray/) maybe not.) According to the web-page markup, the headline font is supposed to be the [Gotham knockoff](typography-2020.html#the-gotham-administration) [Montserrat,](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Montserrat) but apparently someone forgot to upload it—or walked off the job before they could—so Helvetica is being displayed instead. The other font is the Gotham knockoff [Metropolis.](https://www.1001fonts.com/metropolis-font.html) No idea why one Gotham knockoff wouldn’t have sufficed. Or one <span class="no-hyphens">term.</span>

<div id="nikki-haley" class="subhead">

[Nikki Haley](#nikki-haley)

</div>

[](//nikkihaley.com)

Nikki Haley is currently [polling at 4% nationally,](https://projects.fivethirtyeight.com/polls/president-primary-r/2024/national/) yet her home page depicts her as one small face in a crowd. All she’s missing is a [Where’s Waldo](https://www.walmart.com/ip/Women-s-Where-s-Waldo-Wenda-Costume/534535101) outfit. I mentioned the red–blue contrast problem—Haley’s logo is hopeless because every time it sits atop blue or gray, the “Haley” largely disappears. The whole thing just comes across as a cry for help. The fonts are the Google-made [Roboto](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Roboto) and the Russian-made [Futura <span class="no-hyphens">PT.</span>](https://www.paratype.com/fonts/pt/futura-pt)

<div id="vivek-ramaswamy" class="subhead">

[Vivek Ramaswamy](#vivek-ramaswamy)

</div>

[](//vivek2024.com)

Vivek Ramaswamy is the fictional presidential candidate I would expect to find in a Grand Theft Auto game. Though considering he did [pay off a Wikipedia editor,](https://www.forbes.com/sites/mattnovak/2023/05/03/wikipedia-editor-says-they-were-paid-to-change-vivek-ramaswamys-page/) placing his product into a video game might’ve been money better spent. To be fair, I rather like his strange logo, which I choose to read as a secret homage to that of [Van Halen.](http://www.van-halen.com/) Thus, a missed opportunity to deploy the slogan [*(A Different Kind of) Truth.*](https://www.vhnd.com/a-different-kind-of-truth/) The display font is the bland but well-made [Acumin;](https://fonts.adobe.com/fonts/acumin) text is the even blander [Open <span class="no-hyphens">Sans.</span>](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Open+Sans)

<div id="asa-hutchinson" class="subhead">

[Asa Hutchinson](#asa-hutchinson)

</div>

[](//asa2024.com)

Asa? Wowza. Some candidates want to end America’s forever wars. Hutchinson apparently plans to open a new front against the English language itself. On the other hand, the previous administration wanted us to [“Be Best”.](https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/bebest/) Perhaps “For America’s Best” is the natural next step. The website blends the 1970s classic [Grouch](https://fontsinuse.com/typefaces/7277/itc-grouch) with possible future classic [Glacial Indifference.](https://www.fontsquirrel.com/fonts/glacial-indifference) (Based on those font names, I’m detecting that Hutchinson may be the victim of a prank by a web designer less loyal than [Brad Parscale.](#donald-trump)) For good measure, there’s also some [TNR](https://typographyforlawyers.com/a-brief-history-of-times-new-roman.html) in the logo. I’m a little surprised that Hutchinson chose not to align himself with another former Arkansas governor, [Bill Clinton,](https://www.amazon.com/One-Left-Lie-Triangulations-Jefferson/dp/1455522996) who ascended to the presidency and then delivered on many Republican legislative <span class="no-hyphens">priorities.</span>

<div id="larry-elder" class="subhead">

[Larry Elder](#larry-elder)

</div>

[](//larryelder.com)

Larry Elder’s site distinguishes itself by not being completely inept. It’s a low bar this year. Like [Joe Biden,](#joe-biden) the stripes in his logo come across as baconesque, or maybe streaks left by a star-shaped sponge on a windshield. ([Ron DeSantis](#ron-desantis) shows how to render stripes correctly.) Mostly it’s a problem of scale, however: there’s not enough room in the corner for that kind of detail to be legible. The boring free fonts are [Open Sans](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Open+Sans) and [Roboto.](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Roboto)

<div id="tim-scott" class="subhead">

[Tim Scott](#tim-scott)

</div>

[](//votetimscott.com)

Tim Scott apparently took a break from his vacation to announce that yeah, what the heck—why not run for president? I haven’t ruled out the possibility that he took this photo with a selfie stick. As for the logo, the campaign team apparently said “Has anyone in Congress named Tim run recently? Oh really, [Democrat Tim Ryan?](typography-2020.html#tim-ryan) He’s not using his logo anymore, right?” Scott is using one of the better fonts seen in a campaign this year, called [Rift.](https://fortfoundry.com/fonts/rift)

<div id="ron-desantis" class="subhead">

[Ron DeSantis](#ron-desantis)

</div>

[](//rondesantis.com)

I feel a little bad for Ron DeSantis. Like [Nikki Haley,](#nikki-haley) actually being a presidential candidate seems to enchant him less than cosplaying one did. The website looks like the default theme that shipped with WordPress in 2009, with a strange gradient background that blends deep red and blue to make deep sludge. The logo is pinched from Netflix (by way of [Pete Buttigieg](typography-2020.html#pete-buttigieg)). The good news: the stripes actually look wavy; the bad news: they cast us into an uncanny valley where the US only has three states, represented by those minuscule stars. Unlike other candidates, the DeSantis website specifies no fonts, instead relying on the system fonts on each device. This is mystifying, yet <span class="no-hyphens">perfect.</span>

<div id="mike-pence" class="subhead">

[Mike Pence](#mike-pence)

</div>

[](//mikepence2024.com)

Pence’s [capsule biography](https://mikepence2024.com/about/michael-r-pence/) completely omits everything that happened between January 2017 and January 2021, inclusive. Accentuate the positive, I guess? Pence’s website also omits any mention of where he stands on the issues. Fortunately it does include one of his [wedding photos.](https://mikepence2024.com/about/michael-r-pence/) Pence may want to be careful with the name-in-box logo, a motif similar to that of a candidate whose name Pence would prefer I not mention, but it rhymes with [Tronald Dump.](#donald-trump) Display font is [Antonia;](https://typejockeys.com/font/antonia) text is the Russian-made [Futura <span class="no-hyphens">PT.</span>](https://www.paratype.com/fonts/pt/futura-pt)

<div id="chris-christie" class="subhead">

[Chris Christie](#chris-christie)

</div>

[](//chrischristie.com)

“Because the Truth Matters”—according to the man who claims he had nothing to do with traffic-based retribution against the mayor of [Fort Lee, New Jersey](https://www.nytimes.com/2016/11/04/nyregion/george-washington-bridge-scandal-what-you-need-to-know.html) in 2013 that led to federal convictions for two of his aides (later overturned on appeal). Speaking as a New Hampshire native, I can also lay some truth on you, bro—you’re not going to make it to the second primary. Font is [Proxima <span class="no-hyphens">Nova.</span>](https://www.marksimonson.com/fonts/view/proxima-nova)

<div id="doug-burgum" class="subhead">

[Doug Burgum](#doug-burgum)

</div>

[](//dougburgum.com)

Burgum is a peculiar candidate with a peculiar website. Thousands of copies of the message “Text DOUG to 70177” plod endlessly across the top, as if once weren’t enough. The rest of the website scrolls horizontally when you try to scroll vertically, for no good reason. Burgum at least tries to squeeze the actual US flag into his logo rather than baconize it like [Larry Elder.](#larry-elder) I also like that he & his wife picked out color-appropriate outfits for the photo shoot. The display font is the playful and good-natured [Bourton.](https://www.myfonts.com/collections/bourton-font-kimmy-design?tab=individualStyles)

<div id="francis-suarez" class="subhead">

[Francis Suarez](#francis-suarez)

</div>

[](//fxs24.com)

The idea of going jogging with a presidential candidate is not hugely appealing to me. Though Miami mayor Francis Suarez is not the first to try it—see also [Wayne Messam,](typography-2020.html#wayne-messam) another Floridian who campaigned in 2020. As journalists have noted, Suarez doesn’t actually [do much all day](https://www.nytimes.com/2023/06/15/us/politics/francis-suarez-president.html) except post spicy tweets about cryptocurrency. Which immediately makes him a 2024 frontrunner. Designers for [Ron DeSantis](#ron-desantis) take note—this background gradient uses lighter, more saturated colors on the edges so the middle doesn’t get sludgy. Considering this page looks like it took about 25 minutes to make—including a logo concept stolen from [Trump](#donald-trump)—it’s not bad. It remains to be seen, however, what exactly Suarez is going to help us “get started”, but if it’s anything other than “this party”, I [will be very disappointed.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QRINgISPUWQ) Display font is [Montserrat;](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Montserrat) text is <span class="no-hyphens">Verdana.</span>

<div id="green-candidate" class="subhead">

[Green candidate](#green-candidate)

</div>

<div id="cornel-west" class="subhead">

[Cornel West](#cornel-west)

</div>

[](//cornelwest24.com)

West seems to be channeling the monochromatic stylings of [Beto O’Rourke,](typography-2020.html#beto-orourke) who lost consecutive races for US Senate, US President, and Texas governor. Interesting model to follow. Still, West is a cool dude and if he gets on a debate stage, I will watch. The blah font is [Roboto.](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Roboto)

—Matthew Butterick
