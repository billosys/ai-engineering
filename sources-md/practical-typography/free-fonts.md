Hundreds of free fonts have arrived in recent years. Not just “free” as in “already installed on your computer” (aka <a href="system-fonts.html" class="xref">system fonts</a>). But rather, released under an open license, most often the [OFL,](http://scripts.sil.org/ofl) which permits you to do almost anything you want with them. Most importantly, you can redistribute copies for <span class="no-hyphens">free.</span>

At times I’ve been labeled a free-font curmudgeon. My beef with free fonts is not that they’re useless. On the contrary, fonts without proprietary licensing restrictions fill a need, most of all as a complement to open-source software. For instance, I couldn’t have redesigned the [documentation for Racket,](https://docs.racket-lang.org/) an open-source programming language, without free fonts, because we had to ship them with the <span class="no-hyphens">software.</span>

Instead, my complaint about free fonts has centered on a simpler issue: in terms of design and craftsmanship, most [free fonts are garbage.](why-google-fonts-arent-really-open-source.md) Sure, that’s true of many professional fonts too. But early on, certain free-font proponents pushed the argument that somehow, [quantity mattered more than <span class="no-hyphens">quality.</span>](http://typedrawers.com/discussion/comment/1827/#Comment_1827)

Fortunately, not everyone felt the same way. Across my years [raging](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3L4YrGaR8E4) against this particular machine, organizations that appreciate the value of good design—and had money to spend—funded the development of some free fonts that are actually very <span class="no-hyphens">good:</span>

- Adobe released a very nice <a href="monospaced-fonts.html" class="xref">monospaced font</a> called <a href="fontrec/source-code.html" class="xref">Source Code</a>, designed by Paul Hunt; and the companion <a href="fontrec/source-serif.html" class="xref">Source Serif</a>, designed by Frank <span class="no-hyphens">Grießhammer.</span>

- The Smithsonian commissioned [Chester Jenkins](https://www.bspk.xyz/) to create the excellent <a href="fontrec/cooper-hewitt.html" class="xref">Cooper Hewitt</a> sans serif <span class="no-hyphens">family.</span>

- Most recently, IBM’s Mike Abbink worked with [Paul van der Laan](https://www.boldmonday.com/) to make <a href="fontrec/ibm-plex.html" class="xref">IBM Plex</a>, an immense family covering sans, serif, and <span class="no-hyphens">mono.</span>

- Since I launched *Practical Typography*, I’ve been pushing <a href="charter.html" class="xref">Charter</a>, a 1987 Matthew Carter design that holds up beautifully on today’s <span class="no-hyphens">screens.</span>

The fonts above are wonderful! And wonderfully free! Whether my curmudgeonisms have had an impact, I can’t say. But today, if your project requires free fonts, you have plenty of good options. If you refuse, you have no one to blame but <span class="no-hyphens">yourself.</span>

As for the gazillions of other free fonts—if you’ve got the patience to pan for gold in a [river of crap,](effluents-influence-affluence.html#traffic) knock yourself out. But unless your time has no value, you’ll find it more efficient just to buy a nice professional font (see <a href="font-recommendations.html" class="xref">font recommendations</a>).

<div class="btw">

<div class="btw-title">

by the way

</div>

- Why haven’t I listed any <a href="system-fonts.html" class="xref">system fonts</a> above? Careful—they’re not the same thing. System fonts are only “free” in the sense that you don’t have to pay extra for them. But they’re not “free” in the deeper sense of having minimal licensing restrictions. On the contrary, the fonts that come with Windows or Mac OS are governed by a proprietary license which permits certain uses and prohibits <span class="no-hyphens">others.</span>

- “But have you considered my favorite free font, Ludicrous Sans?” If it’s your favorite, my opinion hardly matters. In any case, I’m not a reviewer of free fonts. There are far too many, and I care far too <span class="no-hyphens">little.</span>
